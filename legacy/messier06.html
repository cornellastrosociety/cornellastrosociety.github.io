Messier Marathon 2006

The second CAS Messier marathon of recent history was attempted on March 27/28 at Fuertes. The instruments used were the 12-inch refractor and the 11 x 80 binoculars. Art and Mike participated; while Shianne and Bernadette showed up around 9:15pm, stayed for about 90 minutes, and made good faith efforts, they were unable to locate any objects that had not already been found.

Between cloudy nights and miscommunications on one clear night, we had only weekdays left, which meant no chance to get the late-night objects if we were to be functional at school/work the next day. To compensate, we logged several non-Messier objects along the way. Also, since only 2 people were taking part, we divided/optimized the work: because Mike was more experienced at using the 'scope, he stayed on it and concentrated on finding fainter objects. Meanwhile, Art did dome-moving/weight-winding duties and logged as many objects by binoculars as possible, to buy Mike more time for each harder object. Even so, we missed some early-evening objects that were either too washed-out by light pollution or too low: M33, M77, M79, M110. (We did, at least, bag M74, which had been missed in the 2004 attempt.)

Art had to give up around 1am, by which time we had logged some 50-odd objects. Mike continued on solo until a bit after 3am; the tedium of finding objects while having to mind the dome/weight, with no one else there to share the beauty/wonder of it, finally proved too much. (It was also becoming cloudy.) The final tally was 70 out of 110 Messier objects, plus 9 non-Messier objects.

Missing objects

Most of the missed objects were the pre-dawn items in Aquarius and Capricorn, a scattering of things primarily in Ophiuchus and Serpens Caput/Cauda, and items close to the horizon in Scorpius and Sagittarius. (Because of tree-growth over the last 20-30 years, the window for finding Scorpius/Sagittarius objects from Fuertes on any given night is rather short.) If we had not been attempting the marathon on a weekday, so that we could have been more gung-ho and others could have participated and helped with dome/weight duties, it is likely that we would have been able to push the final tally close to 90, even discounting the non-Messier objects.

There's always next year!
