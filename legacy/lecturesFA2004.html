Cornell Astronomical Society

CAS Lectures Fall 2004

The CAS Lecture Series is an opportunity for the general public to learn more about astronomy than the weekly observing sessions permit. The series consists of several talks given by CAS members or outside speakers on a variety of topics in astronomy or space exploration. The lectures are held on Fridays throughout the fall and spring semesters in the first floor classroom at Fuertes. Little or no astronomy background is required.

September 17

Solar System Ambassador Talk: Cassini Around Saturn

Tony Pilato

Mr. Pilato is a science educator and a Solar System Ambassador with extensive knowledge in astronomy and astrophotography. He will speak on the latest results from the Cassini Mission around Saturn and the future of the mission, including the Huygen's probe that will explore Saturn's moon, Titan.

Extrasolar Planets

November 5

Unsteady Lights in the Heavens: A Talk on Variable Stars

Richard "Doc" Kinne

Kinne will go over the history of variable star astronomy, the different types of variable stars that inhabit the universe, and how you can observe these performers of the stellar sphere and have your observations used by professional astronomers across the world.

November 19

Stars: Their Birth, Life, and Death

Jagadheep Pandian

The lecture will illustrate how stars are born, how they live, and how they die. Cool pictures of star forming nebulae, planetary nebulae and supernovae are guaranteed!
