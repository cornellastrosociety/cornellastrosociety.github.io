Cornell Astronomical Society

What we saw, 2004-2005

All observations were made through the 12" refractor unless otherwise specified.

Friday, July 29, 2005

Mostly clear skies early on gave rise to mostly cloudy skies (altocumulus). Large crowd early on, treated to excellent viewing of Jupiter. Other objects: M13, M57, Albireo, Dumbbell Nebula, M14 (barely visible) and M15. A few very nice meteors as well, one of which passed the finder scope's field of view.

Friday, July 22, 2005

Large crowd visited for Friday night open house. Conditions were mostly clear with occasional low clouds. Objects included the usual favorites: Jupiter, M57, M13, and for a family interested in colors, Albireo and Mu Cephei. Also, a glimpse at a nearly full waning moon.
Note: we must keep the logbook closed from now on to prevent, ahem, "entries" by guests.

Wednesday, July 13, 2005

Opened at 9:30pm for a group of 22 kids. Showed Jupiter (2 moons visible) and the Moon. Went on to Cassiopeia clusters after the folks left: NGCs 7789 (magnificent cluster), 7790, 7788, 457 (ET Cluster), 663, and M103. Mike stopped by around midnight.

Tuesday, July 12, 2005

Open for CAU stargazing. Very hazy but observed Venus, lunar craters, and Jupiter. Great pass of the ISS at 10:42pm--very bright (mag. -0.3), and crossed the entire sky.

Friday, June 24, 2005

Clear Friday night, save a few cirrus low on the horizon. A large, diverse multi-national crowd visited for pre-moonrise observations of: Venus, Saturn, and Mercury all in one binocular field of view, Jupiter, M12, M27, M57, M80, and M4*.
(* First time observed at Fuertes by the three officers--Jagadheep, Mike, Guanhan--, the notoriously elusive cluster was located by Mike.)

Friday, May 27, 2005

Mostly clear Friday night. Seeing good near zenith; bad at about 45 degrees elevation. Objects: Saturn (Titan, Rhea could be seen), Jupiter, M57, M51, ε Lyrae.

Friday, May 6, 2005

Clear Friday night. Atmosphere transparency not good; seeing not good. A group of elementary students and teachers dropped by. Objects: Saturn (could not see Cassini Division due to seeing), Jupiter (could see Big Red Spot), M81, M82, M5, M104.

Saturday, April 16, 2005

Clear Friday [sic] night. First quarter moon phase. Objects: Saturn, Jupiter, Moon, M3.

Saturday-Sunday, April 9-10, 2005

Clear Saturday night. Pon and Mike attempted a mini Messier marathon, but didn't log in times. M-objects found: 42, 43, 36, 37, 38, 35, 100, 99, 88, 91, 90, 89, 58, 59, 60, 87, 44, 45. M-objects looked for but not found: 78, 1, 98, 86.

Friday, April 8, 2005

Clear Friday night. Pon gave a lecture on Cosmology at 8:00pm. Objects: Orion Nebula, Jupiter, Saturn. Var. star estimates of α Orionis and U Monocerotis.

Friday, March 25, 2005

Partly cloudy Friday night. (Spring break.) There were quite a few visitors. Objects looked at: Saturn, Moon, Jupiter, M37.

Friday, March 4, 2005

Mostly clear Friday night. Mike opened alone and was accidentally locked out of the dome when, having left his keys on the eyepiece case, the wind blew the door shut as he was outside shoveling snow. A kind visitor provided him a ride to Krzysztof's dorm to borrow his key. Let this be a lesson to those observing alone.

Targets: Saturn, Jupiter, Orion Nebula.

Friday, February 4, 2005

First observing night of the year, Partly cloudy, then hazy. Opened observatory at 8:15 and tested repairs to telescope RA controls. Telescope worked fine but some eyepieces had dewed over. Pointed telescope at Saturn. At 9:00 haze moved in. Saturn could be seen through telescope but not naked eye, so changing targets impossible. Total of 3 visitors. Closed at 10:00.

Friday, December 17, 2004

Last day of exams. Partly cloudy, then mostly clear. Guanhan opened at 8pm and showed objects like Saturn, the Orion Nebula, M38, and M36. Most visitors were townies. Jagadheep came later, when Guanhan was looking for Comet Macholz, which didn't show signs of a tail. Other objects seen: M79, M1, M78 (finally!). Total of 10-20 visitors. Closed at midnight.

Friday, December 3, 2004

First, had CAS meeting regarding Spring '05 lectures (Doc, Debbie attended meeting, then left). Afterwards opened for public (partly cloudly skies). Objects looked at: Double Cluster, Gamma Andromedae, M38. Had lively argument on what M38 looks like. Clouded up at 10pm and closed up at 10:30.

Friday, November 26, 2004

Partly cloudy Friday night (~cloudy at 8pm, ~clear at 8:30). Krzysztof opened observatory at 8:30; Jagadheep and Mike showed up at 10:15. Objects observed: Moon (full; observed with variable polarization filter), NGC 7789, NGC 663. 10-15 visitors tonight (day after Thanskgiving).

Friday, November 12, 2004

Clear Friday night (slightly cloudy in south). Objects looked at: Double Cluster, Pleiades, M77 (NGC 1068), M38, Saturn (turbulent, but Cassini Division, atmospheric band could be seen).

Friday, November 5, 2004

Doc lectured on variable stars to about 24 people. Quasi-clear skies; so public observation night. Targets: M15, M37. Clouded up at 10:30pm.

Wednesday, October 27, 2004

Public opening for total lunar eclipse. Mostly clear sky, with few passing altocumulus clouds. Totality lasted from 10:23pm to 11:45pm. Large crowd despite competition with Boston's World Series victory.

Friday, October 22, 2004

Public observation night. Mostly clear, but moist. Moon, first quarter. Objects: Moon, E.T. Cluster, M57 (Ring). Krzysztof lectured on extra-solar planets to a full crowd.

Saturday, October 9, 2004

Practice night. Found M13, then aborted at 10:00 due to cloudy weather. A group of four dropped by and we answered some general questions.

Friday, October 8, 2004

Public observation night. Scattered cirrus clouds. Small crowd due to fall break. Objects: M15, Albireo, Perseus Double Cluster. Finished session before moonrise. Closed a half hour early.

Friday, October 1, 2004

Public observation night. Scattered cirrus clouds. A bit chilly; a large crowd tonight. Objects: M15, M57 (Ring Nebula), E.T. Cluster, bright gibbous (waning) moon (Pon looks at moon with naked eye and complains it is bright!), M2. Mike closes on his own--training almost complete.

Friday, September 24, 2004

Public observation night. Scattered clouds. Bright gibbous moon. Trained scope on β Cygni, the moon, and Uranus. Set the binoculars on the deck and shown them on the moon and the Pleiades.

Saturday, September 11, 2004

Held an instruction session for the Astro Department TAs. Went over dome opening and closing procedures and also practiced finding various bright objects: Vega, β Lyrae, M13, M27, M57.

The clock drive has ceased working completely and Jagadheep and the TAs are working at its repair.

Friday, September 10, 2004

Public viewing night. Perhaps 80 people in attendance through the night. Continued to have problems with the clock drive. Jagadheep has made some progress for getting it fixed.

Concentrated the scope on M31, M32, M17, M15 (globular cluster in Pegasus, containing a planetary nebula), and γ Andromedae.

Saturday, September 4, 2004

Small class to teach the new members the operation of the scope. Trained scope on Vega, Altair, γ Lyrae and the area of M51. Had a few visitors walk in and look about.

Friday, September 3, 2004

Regular public night. Was largely cloudly but we started out with β Cygni, moved on to M15, and ended up with the Ranger 9 crash site on the Moon. (Debris was not obviously visible...)
