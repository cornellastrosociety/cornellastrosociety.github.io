The Cornell Astronomical Society & Fuertes Observatory

The Cornell Astronomical Society (CAS) has conducted public viewing nights at Cornell University's Fuertes Observatory for over 50 years. We open the observatory every Friday night during the academic year from 8 PM until midnight, regardless of clouds.

When clear, public viewing takes place through the observatory's telescopes, including the historic 12" Irving Porter Church Refractor. Tours of the historic building, the Irving Porter Church Refractor, and our museum will be given throughout the night, regardless of the weather.

During Cornell winter and summer breaks, check this page to see if we're open. Join our listserv at the bottom of this page for weekly updates on events!

Visit our Hours & Directions page for directions and parking information, and the Open House and Accessibility pages for what to expect when here.

Lectures to the public are given by Cornell astronomy faculty and other researchers several times throughout the semester on Friday nights, prior to the open house. Visit the Lecture Series page for a list of upcoming lectures.

Joining the Cornell Astronomical Society

The Cornell Astronomical Society is Cornell's astronomy club. All Cornell students, regardless of prior experience or area of study, are welcome to become members of CAS. Visit About the Club for more information.

Fuertes Observatory Tours and Group Events

Organizations and informal large groups may request an educational observing session at Fuertes Observatory conducted by CAS volunteers. These can be arranged on non-Fridays and any night when the observatory is not reserved for introductory astronomy classes. Private sessions are completely free! Scheduling of events is subject to the availability of CAS officers and to the full discretion of the Cornell Department of Astronomy. Send us an email to arrange an event.
