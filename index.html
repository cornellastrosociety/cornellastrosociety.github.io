Total Lunar Eclipse Information
If clear, the Cornell Astronomical Society will be opening the Fuertes Observatory for observing of the total lunar eclipse, in the early morning of Tuesday, March 3rd, 2026. More information is presented below.
If clouds prevent your viewing of the moon, we cannot see it either, and may not be open! Please ensure the cloud cover is reasonable before considering stopping by.

If clear, the Fuertes Observatory will be open to the public from 5:30 AM to 7 AM on Tuesday morning, March 3rd, for the observing of the upcoming total eclipse of the Moon. The eclipse occurs in several phases:
  • 3:44:25 AM: Start of penumbral eclipse. The Moon becomes very slightly dimmer, as it is shadowed by Earth's penumbra.
  • 4:50 AM: Start of partial eclipse. Earth's umbral shadow begins to be cast upon the Moon, significantly dimming a growing portion of the Moon.
  • 6:04 AM: Start of total eclipse. The Moon is completely within Earth's umbral shadow, and begins to turn reddish.
  • 6:40:03 AM: End of eclipse; the moon sets and the sun rises.
Unlike a solar eclipse, there is no danger to observing the moon during an eclipse without specialized equipment or filters, so one does not need any sort of eclipse glasses to view this event. Observation of the eclipse is dependent on the weather; if the skies are overcast and the moon cannot be seen, then the eclipse will not be visible either. Comprehensive plots regarding the weather at Fuertes and the surrounding area, including cloud cover, can be found on our Weather page.

The Cornell Astronomical Society & Fuertes Observatory

The Cornell Astronomical Society (CAS) has conducted public viewing nights at Cornell University's Fuertes Observatory for over 50 years. We open the observatory every Friday night during the academic year from 8 PM until midnight, regardless of clouds.

When clear, public viewing takes place through the observatory's telescopes, including the historic 12" Irving Porter Church Refractor. Tours of the historic building, the Irving Porter Church Refractor, and our museum will be given throughout the night, regardless of the weather.

During Cornell winter and summer breaks, check this page to see if we're open. Join our listserv at the bottom of this page for weekly updates on events!

Visit our Hours & Directions page for directions and parking information, and the Open House and Accessibility pages for what to expect when here.

Lectures to the public are given by Cornell astronomy faculty and other researchers several times throughout the semester on Friday nights, prior to the open house. Visit the Lecture Series page for a list of upcoming lectures.

All events hosted by the Cornell Astronomical Society are free and open to the public. If you would like to contribute to programming at the Fuertes Observatory and astronomy outreach at Cornell, you are welcome to leave a donation. Gifts to the Cornell Astronomical Society can be made in-person using the donation box on our front table. If you would prefer to donate by mail, please email us at astrosociety@cornell.edu for more information.

Joining the Cornell Astronomical Society

The Cornell Astronomical Society is Cornell's astronomy club. All Cornell students, regardless of prior experience or area of study, are welcome to become members of CAS. Visit About the Club for more information.

Fuertes Observatory Tours and Group Events

Organizations and informal large groups may request an educational observing session at Fuertes Observatory conducted by CAS volunteers. These can be arranged on non-Fridays and any night when the observatory is not reserved for introductory astronomy classes. Private sessions are completely free! Scheduling of events is subject to the availability of CAS officers and to the full discretion of the Cornell Department of Astronomy. Send us an email to arrange an event.
