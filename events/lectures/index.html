Cornell Orchestras Information
The Cornell Astronomical Society is working with Cornell Orchestras and the Carl Sagan Institute to host a special lecture, solar observing, and viewing night, all free and open to the public, in connection with the Cornell Symphony Orchestra's annual Young Person's Concert.
Finding Our Way: Traditional Polynesian Celestial Navigation
Appel Commons Multipurpose Room 303 (Directions)
Ho'okele Ka'iulani Murphy
February 28th, 2025,
7 PM–8 PM
Appel Commons Multipurpose Room 303
Ho'okele Ka'iulani Murphy, Professor of Hawaiian Astronomy and Navigation at University of Hawai'i Honolulu Community College, will be sharing the story of Hōkūle'a, the voyaging canoe at the heart of the revival of traditional navigation in Hawai'i. Guided by the knowledge of the heavens and the ocean, contemporary navigators sail in the wake of their ancestors.
Friday Open House at the Fuertes Observatory
February 28th, 2025,
8 PM–12 AM
Fuertes Observatory
Following the lecture by Appel Commons, the Fuertes Observatory will be open to the public for observing and tours of the historic telescope, artifacts, and facility. The Observatory will close as usual, at midnight.
Solar Observing at Bailey Hall
March 2nd, 2025,
2 PM-3 PM
Bailey Hall
Preceding the concert by the Cornell Symphony Orchestra, the Cornell Astronomical Society will be operating a solar telescope outside of Bailey Hall, dependent on the weather and the skies.
Special Sunday Open House at the Fuertes Observatory
March 2nd, 2025,
6 PM
Fuertes Observatory
Following the concert by the Cornell Symphony Orchestra, the Fuertes Observatory will open to the public for a special Sunday open house beginning at 6 PM, for observing and tours.
