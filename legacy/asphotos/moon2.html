Lunar Mosaic

Mike Roman created this mosaic by stitching together 12 seperate contrast-enhanced images he made through our main telescope using his Canon 300D digital camera (the exposures were 1/60 of a second at 100 ISO for you technical folks). The result is an extremely detailed image of the moon as it appears about 2 days past full.

In fact, the mosaic is too detailed to show here in its full glory -- each dimension has been shrunk by a factor of 4 to keep loading and display times reasonable. If you want, we also have the full, 11 MB version. If you are using Internet Explorer and the image fits in your browser window, you should click the "Expand to regular size" button floating over the lower right corner.
